About

SSAGES (Software Suite for Advanced General Ensemble Simulations) provides a unified, extensible framework adaptable to calculate reaction coordinates and reactive pathways, and free energies within generic molecular simulations.



MICCoM codes SSAGES, COPSS, and DASH are open-source and distributed under the GNU General Public License (v3.0). These codes were developed at Argonne National Laboratory in conjunction with the University of Chicago and the University of Notre Dame.

MICCoM also supports the development of WEST and Qbox.




Seamless Integration

SSAGES is designed from the ground up to connect to arbitrary molecular simulation codes through minimal programming effort. Initial releases target specific interoperability with open-source codes GROMACS and LAMMPS, and will grow to incorporate other open-source, proprietary, and bespoke codes.



SSAGES

SSAGES implements several methods for reactive pathway identification and free energy measurement, including Forward Flux Sampling, Metadynamics, and Nudged Elastic Band methods.
